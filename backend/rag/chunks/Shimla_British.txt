Shimla, British Raj (c. 1900 CE)

Political & Security:
The area of present-day Shimla was invaded and captured by Bhimsen Thapa of Nepal in 1806.

Economy & Trade:
Once recruited to IAAS, the directly recruited officers are trained mainly at the National Academy of Audit and Accounts.
Banks in Scotland and Northern Ireland retain the right to issue their own notes, subject to retaining enough Bank of England notes in reserve to cover the issuance.

Daily Life & Culture:
Jakhoo Temple, for the Hindu god Hanuman, is located at the highest point in Shimla.
Another popular temple in the city is Kamna Devi Temple, situated atop of Prospect Hill, one of the seven hills of Shimla.
