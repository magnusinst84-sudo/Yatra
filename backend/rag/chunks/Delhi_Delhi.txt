Delhi, Delhi Sultanate (c. 1350 CE)

Economy & Trade:
As with other regions of India, the IT industry has been expanding in Delhi.

Architecture & Cityscape:
The architecture of Delhi dates back more than a thousand years.
After Delhi was declared the site for a new capital of India, George V laid the foundation of New Delhi, which would serve as the capital.

Daily Life & Culture:
Other monuments include the India Gate, the Jantar Mantar—an 18th-century astronomical observatory—and the Purana Qila—a 16th-century fortress.
Over the centuries, Delhi has become known for its composite culture, and a festival that symbolises this is the Phool Walon Ki Sair, which takes place in September.
