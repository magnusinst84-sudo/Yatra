Rajgir, Mahajanapadas (c. 500 BCE)

Architecture & Cityscape:
A Śvētāmbara Jaina temple located at the foothills of Rājagṛha, this temple is also known as "Gāon Mandira" (village temple).
The principal deity of this temple is a black-coloured stone idol of Munisuvrata.

Daily Life & Culture:
Both Mahavira and Buddha taught their beliefs in Rajgir during the 6th and 5th century BC, and the Buddha was offered a forest monastery here by the king Bimbisara.
As such, the city of Rajgir became one of the Buddha's most important preaching locations.
