Delhi, Indian Independence (1947)

Economy & Trade:
Delhi is the largest commercial center in northern India.
Delhi's large consumer market and the availability of skilled labour has also attracted foreign investment.

Daily Life & Culture:
Three World Heritage Sites—the Red Fort, Qutub Minar and Humayun's Tomb—are located in Delhi.
New Delhi houses several government buildings and official residences reminiscent of British colonial architecture, including the Rashtrapati Bhavan, the Secretariat, Rajpath, the Parliament of India and Vijay Chowk.
